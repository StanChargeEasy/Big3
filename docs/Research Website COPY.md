# Big-3 Teaser Copy Research — Copy Basis, Best-Practice Examples & SEO Opportunity (DE / ES / EN)
*Prepared for Gabriela (copywriter). Date: 2026-06-07. All sample copy below is fully original, written for this brief. No competitor text is reproduced.*

## TL;DR
- **Adopt Option B (Modular + rules-based synthesis).** No leading consumer astrology product writes 1,728 bespoke triples. The market splits between pure modular delivery (Co-Star, CHANI, AstroSeek, Astrodienst) and a small minority that add ONE pairwise layer (Cafe Astrology's 144 Sun-Ascendant pages). Option B — 36 modular texts + a templated element/modality synthesis close — matches the perceived "bespoke" feel of the best products at a fraction of Option D's cost.
- **The 36-text + synthesis model is exactly what gold-standard sites do.** Astrodienst, Co-Star and CHANI all present placements modularly and read the whole chart through dominance logic (element/modality, chart ruler). Co-Star's own method, per its FAQ, is to use "NASA data to chart the exact location of the stars… then interpreted by real human astrologers who collaborate with AI technology," and Wikipedia specifies the engine "maps human-written snippets of text to planetary movements" — i.e. modular blocks assembled by rule, never bespoke triples.
- **SEO: a zero-authority domain cannot win English head terms** (birth chart calculator KD 80, birth chart KD 89 per Clicks.so). Target mid-difficulty and long-tail clusters per language, and exploit the under-consolidated German "berechnen" calculator SERPs and the region-split Spanish market ("carta astral" Spain vs "carta natal" LatAm).

## Key Findings

1. **Modular is the universal base.** Every named competitor stores one text block per placement and stitches them: Sun block + Moon block + Rising block. Co-Star gives an in-depth description of the trait corresponding to each placement one by one; AstroSeek's "Big Three" tool returns three separate placement readings; Astrodienst (astro.com) returns modular placement interpretations in its free chart.
2. **Synthesis is done by rule, not by hand.** The credible "what this means together" writing in the market is assembled from dominance logic: dominant element (fire/earth/air/water), dominant modality (cardinal/fixed/mutable), and chart-ruler emphasis. Several calculators explicitly compute "elemental and modality distribution" plus a "personality summary" from the three signs rather than storing bespoke triples.
3. **Only ONE named competitor goes pairwise, and only for one pair.** Cafe Astrology publishes a full 12×12 grid of Sun-Ascendant combination pages (e.g. /sun-taurus-ascendant-cancer.html), introduced on its sign-combinations index as exploring "the nuances and common traits of Sun-Ascendant sign combinations," plus separate Sun-Moon "blends." This 144-text layer is the high-water mark the market actually reaches — never the full 1,728.
4. **CHANI and Co-Star both privilege Rising for "live" content but present all three modularly for the natal read.** CHANI's own help docs state "the rising sign becomes primary, with the Sun secondary and the Moon tertiary," and that "all of our content is written and recorded by Chani Nicholas and our team of professional astrologers" — human-written, rising-led, no AI, no combination-specific natal essays at scale. This confirms the modular-plus-rules pattern even at the premium end.
5. **Length/tone benchmarks:** competitor placement blurbs cluster in two tiers — short app-card blurbs (~30–60 words, e.g. Co-Star/calculators) and longer editorial paragraphs (~150–250 words, e.g. Cafe Astrology's Moon-in-Scorpio essay). The brief's 60–90 word target sits deliberately between them: richer than an app card, more scannable than an editorial essay.
6. **Mobile UX converges on progressive disclosure + cards.** The dominant pattern is stacked card modules with a short headline/summary visible and "read full" expansion — exactly what the CHANI redesign case study recommends ("summaries first, depth on demand"), and what app-UX guides advise ("daily readings work well as swipeable cards; long reports split into short sections").
7. **Market scale validates the investment.** Co-Star alone reached roughly 30 million registered users by July 2023 (up ~4× from 7.4 million in November 2020, per Statista/Co-Star data) — the most widely adopted Western astrology app and clear evidence the Big-3 consumer category is large and growing.

### C. The Synthesis Spec (how to write the close WITHOUT 1,728 texts)

**Mechanism:** assemble the synthesis paragraph from a small library of ~12–24 connective fragments selected by three computed signals:

1. **Dominant element** across Sun+Moon+Rising (ideally the full chart). Rules:
 - 2–3 of the same element → "lead" fragment for that element (Fire = drive/passion; Earth = grounded/practical; Air = ideas/social; Water = emotional/intuitive).
 - All three different → "blend/bridge" fragment ("you move between…").
2. **Dominant modality** (Cardinal = initiator; Fixed = stabilizer/persistent; Mutable = adaptable). Same counting rule.
3. **Sun–Moon tension flag:** same element = "aligned/consistent" fragment; clashing elements (e.g. Fire Sun / Water Moon) = "inner contrast" fragment ("outwardly bold, inwardly tender"). This single rule captures most of the felt "bespoke" nuance — exactly the move competitor explainers make ("fiery Sun but water Moon = passionate but deeply sensitive").

**Worked example of the logic** (Cancer Sun, Scorpio Moon, Aries Rising): two Water placements + one Fire → Water-dominant emotional core behind a Fire mask → fragment set produces: *"People meet your spark first, but your depth runs much deeper than it lets on."* This mirrors the real interpretation pattern in German source Astrodamus, which describes exactly this chart as appearing "energisch, direkt und impulsiv" outwardly while hiding "tiefe Sensibilität."

**Three concrete synthesis examples found in the wild (for reference, NOT to copy):**
- *Virgo Sun / Cancer Moon / Libra Rising* — core identity focused on service and practical improvement, emotional nature sensitive and security-oriented, presenting as socially graceful (calcempire / zodiactalk synthesis pattern).
- *Pisces Sun / Aries Moon / Capricorn Rising* — "blends dreamy idealism with fiery drive, balancing empathy with a strong sense of responsibility" (athrbeauty).
- *Leo Sun / Virgo Moon / Scorpio Rising* — described as a "Researcher/Performer hybrid" (thedigizone). Each example uses element/modality contrast as the engine — the model to template.

### D. Recommended length, tone & structure (per brief, validated against market)

- **Per placement: 60–90 words, 2nd person, warm, premium.** Structure: (1) one-line identity hook → (2) one genuine strength → (3) one growth edge → (4) one relational/everyday note. Richer than Co-Star's app card, tighter than Cafe Astrology's essay — the right "premium but scannable" middle.
- **Synthesis close: 60–100 words**, element/modality-led, naming the felt contrast or harmony.
- **Tone calibration:** avoid fatalism (Astrodienst's standard disclaimer — "the planets do not compel you… you still have the free will to determine your own life" — is a good north star); favor empowerment + specificity. Co-Star's draw is "biting truth"; CHANI's is warmth/healing. Recommend landing closer to CHANI's warmth given the "premium" brief, with Co-Star's specificity.

### F. Mobile UX recommendation

- **Single vertical scroll of stacked cards**, one per placement (Sun → Moon → Rising), each card showing the identity hook + first ~15 words, expanding to full on tap ("progressive disclosure: summaries first, depth on demand").
- **Synthesis card last**, visually distinct ("What this means together") as the payoff/woven close.
- **Element/modality mini-visual** (the dominance bar competitors compute) above or within the synthesis card — high perceived value, near-zero copy cost.
- Keep birth-data entry friendly: explain *why* time/place are needed inline (a documented friction-reducer), since Rising requires exact time.

# Big-3 Teaser Copy — Research Summary

## 1. Structural Approach (Modular vs. Woven vs. Combinatorial)
- Leading astrology sites typically present Sun, Moon, and Rising as **separate sections** (modular). For example, Cafe Astrology’s Aries page has a “Sun in Aries” section (~100 words) and a “Moon in Aries” section (~70 words). CHANI’s “Aries placements” article similarly has distinct “Sun in Aries”, “Moon in Aries”, and “Ascendant in Aries” paragraphs, written in warm, second-person voice. Co–Star’s mobile app also separates traits by placement (though often more list-like).
- Some sources (Astrology.com) do full combinatorial write-ups for each specific Sun/Moon/Rising trio. For example, the “Aries Sun | Aries Moon | Aries Rising” page interweaves all three. This approach (Option D) is rich but scales to ~1,728 texts, which is impractical for our needs.
- **Key finding:** Most public sites favor **modular** (Option A) content for each placement. A small number (like Astrology.com) do full triples. Very few do pairwise (Option C) synthesis. The recommended strategy (Option B) – write separate Sun, Moon, Rising blurbs plus a brief synthesis paragraph – aligns with industry norms. Spanish/German examples echo this: e.g. Miastral’s site explains “Sol, Luna y Ascendente” as key elements, but still would write them up individually. 

## 2. Placement Blurb Style (Length, Tone, Structure)
- **Length:** Existing examples vary, but a typical placement blurb is on the order of 50–150 words. Cafe Astrology’s “Sun in Aries” (~120 words) and “Moon in Aries” (~70 words) illustrate this. CHANI’s paragraphs tend shorter (~50–80 words each). We recommend **60–90 words** per blurb (draft target) to balance depth and conciseness.
- **Tone:** 
  - **Second person, warm:** CHANI consistently uses “you” (“If Aries is your Sun sign, you…”) with a friendly, empowering tone. 
  - **Third person, factual:** CafeAstrology and AstroSeek often use third-person or neutral (“Aries Sun natives are…”). Co–Star uses a modern, direct style. 
  - Our *copy basis* will use second person (“you”) in a premium, supportive voice (no slang, no emojis).
- **Structure:** Each blurb should include: 
    1. **Core Strength** (a positive trait, e.g. Aries = leadership) 
    2. **Growth Edge** (a challenge or shadow side, e.g. Aries = impatience) 
    3. **Relational/Everyday Note** (how it plays out day-to-day or in relationships, e.g. “Your bold energy motivates others”).
  This mirrors patterns seen in examples: CHANI’s “Sun in Aries” emphasizes vitality and courage, and “Moon in Aries” mentions being courageous yet impulsive. 
- **Examples:** Cafe Astrology’s Aries Ascendant blurb (42 words) reads: “People with Aries Ascendants are direct and quick… first instinct is to do, rather than to think”. It identifies a key trait (directness) and a caution (rashness) succinctly. 

## 3. Synthesis/Interplay Guidelines 
- **Modular with a synthesis layer (Option B):** Since writing unique content for each sign triple is infeasible, we’ll write one combined paragraph referencing how the Sun, Moon, and Rising interact. 
- **Elemental logic:** If two or more placements share an element (fire, earth, air, water), highlight that theme. E.g. *“With multiple fire signs, you’re highly passionate and driven”*. Astrology.com’s Aries triple example shows this: “With all three signs in Aries… you are confident, assertive… strong desire for independence”. 
- **Modality logic:** If signs share modality (cardinal, fixed, mutable), note the mutual approach. Cardinal+cardinal might emphasize initiative; multiple fixed = determination; mutable influence adaptability.
- **Balancing combos:** If signs differ, mention how they balance or create tension. For instance, Tarot.com notes: *“An Aries Sun’s impulsivity is tempered by a Virgo Moon or heightened by a Leo Moon”*. We can similarly craft lines like “Your earthy Moon grounds your fiery Sun” or “Your air Rising sign helps you think before you act.”
- **Rule-based assembly:** We might use templated phrases or pivot to the dominant element. Examples (inference): “All three of your signs are fire/water/etc, so this is a super-energized/empathetic chart,” or “You blend the energy of Air (Sun) with Water (Moon), meaning you think things through and feel them deeply.”

## 5. Mobile UX Layout (Big-3 Display)
- **Cards/Swipes:** Many apps (e.g. Co–Star) show each sign on separate “cards” you swipe through. This isolates one placement at a time.
- **Tabs or Progressive Reveal:** Alternatively, a tabbed view or stepwise reveal can let users click “Sun”, then “Moon”, then “Rising”. 
- **Single Scroll:** Putting all three in one scrollable page is also common. If so, we must keep each section short and clearly labeled to avoid overload.
- *Inference (no specific source):* Key is clarity on small screens: big-3 items should not run together. Visually distinct headers (“Sun sign: Aries”, etc.) or dividers help.

## 6. Copy Basis — Draft Guidelines & Samples

- **Voice & Tone:** 2nd-person, warm/premium. Short sentences or clause lists. Avoid jargon.
- **Length:** ~60–90 words each.
- **Structure per blurb:** One or two sentences, each covering a strength, a growth edge, and a relatable note. 
- **Synthesis Blurb:** ~60–100 words, focusing on how the combination plays out (using element/modality logic).
- **Key elements to include:** Sign archetypes (as above), relevant keywords, present-tense active phrasing.
